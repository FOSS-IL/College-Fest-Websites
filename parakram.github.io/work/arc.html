ABOUT TRIVIA FAQ's

“What was magic yesterday is called technology today! Humans now not only think for themselves but make machines ‘think’ as well. This event is all about an autonomous bot built from scratch by participants, judged on its ability to perform usual tasks. It tests the level of precision in the end to end designing and fabrication of the bot, and intelligent use of sensors and coding.”

Rounds and details::

    This event will be conducted in two stages:
  • Qualifiers: In this round, bots will be judged on their ability to solve the given maze or ‘chakravyuh’ in shortest time. Bots will be placed at the starting point in the arena and will have to pass through several checkpoints. Missing checkpoints will result in deduction of points and in case of manhandling, participants will have to start from previous checkpoint. Total score will be formulated by calculating the total time taken by bot, no. of checkpoints passed and total no. of manhandling done.
  • The Final Battle: In this round, with increased levels of difficulty, participants will have to count the blocks placed along their path and follow the wall in order to escape from the ‘Chakravyuh’. In the end, they are supposed to display the total number of blocks they encountered in their way. Team completing the given task successfully in least amount of time will be declared winner.

General Guidelines for the Competition:

  • Maximum of 4 can participate in a team.
  • Team has to be present at the arena 10 minutes before game starts. Absence will result to direct disqualification of the team.
  • Participants are advised to bring their own extensions for power supply.
  • No extra time will be given in case of wire entanglement. Participants are suggested to use wireless power supply for bots.
  • Participants should not disassemble their bots until results are announced.
  • Judges decision will be final.
  • Practice arena will be provided to the participants before the event starts. Once the match get started, no second trial will be given to team.
  • Teams will be given 10 minutes at the time of match to make changes (if any) to their codes.

Judging criteria-

             Efficiency should be maximum.

               e= maximum bearable load/dead weight of bridge

             Deflection in bridge should be minimum.

             Based on two above two factors the winner will be declared.
